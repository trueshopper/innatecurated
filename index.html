THE PROCESS

HOW WE CURATE

The wellness industry is full of products that market well and perform poorly. Proprietary blends that hide underdosed ingredients. Skincare with toxic compounds buried in the fine print. Supplements full of fillers nobody asked for.

We built Innate Curated to be the filter. Every product goes through the same process before it ever appears on this site.

We don't work backwards from commission rates. If a product doesn't pass, it doesn't get featured — regardless of how well it sells.

01

INGREDIENT AUDIT

Every ingredient is checked against known toxin databases and research literature. No shortcuts.

02

LABEL VERIFICATION

We confirm third-party testing certificates and check that labels match what's inside.

03

EFFICACY CHECK

Does the evidence support the claim? We check dosing against clinical research, not marketing copy.

04

RECOMMENDATION

Pass all three? It earns a spot. Fail any one? It doesn't — no exceptions.

OUR CRITERIA

THE STANDARD

CLEAN INGREDIENTS

No artificial dyes, unnecessary fillers, or flow agents. No ingredients linked to hormone disruption, inflammation, or long-term harm.

TRANSPARENT LABELING

No proprietary blends. We need to see exactly what's in the product and at what dose. If a brand is hiding its formula, we're not recommending it.

THIRD-PARTY TESTED

For supplements, we look for NSF Certified, Informed Sport, or USP verification. An independent lab confirms what's on the label is in the product.

EVIDENCE-BASED

Ingredients should have research behind them, dosed at levels that actually do something. No trend ingredients with no clinical backing.

NO GREENWASHING

"Natural," "clean," and "non-toxic" are marketing terms, not standards. We verify claims ourselves — not just the packaging.

REAL EFFICACY

Does it actually work? We look at the mechanism, the dose, and whether the research supports the outcome being claimed.

If a product doesn't meet every one of these criteria, it doesn't appear on this site — regardless of how popular it is, how well it markets, or what the commission rate looks like.
